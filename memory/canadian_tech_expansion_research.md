# Canadian Tech International Expansion Research - Key Insights

## Successful Companies (2023-2025)

### UniUni (Logistics/Delivery)
- **Expansion**: US Market
- **Achievement**: 65% US market coverage, 500+ cities
- **Growth**: 2,000% revenue growth (2022-2025)
- **Funding**: $70M Series D (2024)
- **Key**: Cross-border delivery service US to Canada launched 2025

### ThreatIQ (Cybersecurity) 
- **Expansion**: EU Market (pivoted from US due to market conditions)
- **Achievement**: Hundreds of EU business leads
- **Partnership**: CyberScope Spain
- **Employees**: 35 across Canada
- **Key**: Shifted focus from US to EU for expansion

### Hopper (Travel Tech)
- **Expansion**: Global (US, Japan, EU)
- **Partnerships**: Air Canada, SMCC Japan, Tripadvisor
- **Revenue**: $850M (2024)
- **Funding**: $740M total raised
- **Key**: AI-powered travel booking with fintech products

### Neo Financial (Fintech)
- **Expansion**: Domestic then International
- **Growth**: 1,279% 3-year revenue growth
- **Funding**: $360M Series D (2024)
- **Recognition**: #1 Deloitte Fast 50
- **Key**: Banking revolution, expanded to Toronto

### Additional Companies
- **Videns Analytics**: EU expansion with Airbus contract
- **CoeusAI**: EU market focus for renewable energy AI

## Success Factors
1. Strong Canadian foundation before international expansion
2. Strategic market selection (EU via CETA, US as natural market)
3. Local partnerships crucial for market entry
4. Cultural adaptation and regulatory compliance
5. Focus on solving universal problems with local adaptation

## Market Context
- CETA trade agreement driving EU expansion (71% increase in Canada-EU trade since 2017)
- US market conditions pushing companies toward EU
- EU tech regulations creating opportunities for Canadian innovators
- Focus on AI, cybersecurity, cleantech, fintech sectors
