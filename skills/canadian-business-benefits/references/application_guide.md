# Application Preparation Guide

## General Application Requirements

Most Canadian government business programs require similar core information and documents.

### Business Information

**Required for almost all programs:**

1. **Legal Business Details**
   - Legal business name
   - Operating/Trade name (if different)
   - Business Number (BN) - 9-digit number from CRA
   - Date of incorporation/registration
   - Province/territory of incorporation

2. **Contact Information**
   - Primary contact person
   - Mailing address
   - Phone, email
   - Website (if applicable)

3. **Business Structure**
   - Sole proprietorship
   - Partnership
   - Corporation (specify: private, public, non-profit)
   - Cooperative

4. **Ownership**
   - List of owners/shareholders
   - Ownership percentages
   - Canadian vs. foreign ownership breakdown

### Financial Information

**Commonly requested:**

1. **Current Financial Status**
   - Latest financial statements (balance sheet, income statement)
   - Current year-to-date financials
   - Cash flow statements
   - Banking information

2. **Historical Performance**
   - Previous 2-3 years of financial statements
   - Tax returns (corporate and sometimes personal)
   - Revenue history
   - Employee count history

3. **Financial Projections**
   - 2-5 year revenue projections
   - Expense forecasts
   - Cash flow projections
   - Break-even analysis

### Business Profile

**Standard information:**

1. **Company Overview**
   - Years in operation
   - Number of employees (full-time, part-time, contractors)
   - Annual revenue
   - Industry/sector (NAICS code often required)
   - Products/services offered

2. **Market Information**
   - Target market/customers
   - Geographic reach (local, provincial, national, international)
   - Competitive landscape
   - Market opportunity/size

3. **Operations**
   - Facilities/locations
   - Key equipment/assets
   - Supply chain/partners
   - Intellectual property

## Project-Specific Requirements

For programs supporting specific projects (R&D, expansion, etc.):

### Project Description

**Detailed narrative including:**

1. **Project Overview**
   - Clear, concise summary (1-2 paragraphs)
   - What you're trying to accomplish
   - Why this project matters

2. **Objectives**
   - Specific, measurable goals
   - Expected outcomes
   - Success criteria
   - Timeline and milestones

3. **Methodology/Approach**
   - How you'll execute the project
   - Key activities and tasks
   - Resources required
   - Risk mitigation

4. **Innovation/Uniqueness**
   - What's new or different
   - Competitive advantage
   - Technical innovation (for R&D programs)

### Project Budget

**Detailed breakdown:**

1. **Revenue Sources**
   - Government funding requested
   - Business contribution (cash)
   - In-kind contributions
   - Other funding sources
   - Total project cost

2. **Expense Categories**
   - Salaries and benefits
   - Professional fees (consultants, lawyers, etc.)
   - Equipment and materials
   - Travel and accommodation
   - Marketing and promotion
   - Facilities/rent
   - Other operating costs

3. **Budget Justification**
   - Why each expense is necessary
   - How costs were estimated
   - Quotes for major purchases

### Impact and Benefits

**Expected outcomes:**

1. **Economic Impact**
   - Jobs created or maintained
   - Revenue growth projections
   - Productivity improvements
   - Cost savings

2. **Innovation Impact**
   - New products/services developed
   - Patents or IP generated
   - Technology advancement
   - Process improvements

3. **Market Impact**
   - New markets accessed
   - Market share growth
   - Export sales
   - Customer base expansion

4. **Community/Social Impact**
   - Local economic development
   - Environmental benefits
   - Social value created
   - Knowledge transfer

## Supporting Documents

### Standard Documents

**Almost always required:**

1. **Corporate Documents**
   - Articles of incorporation/partnership agreement
   - Corporate bylaws
   - Shareholder agreement
   - Business license/permits

2. **Financial Documents**
   - Financial statements (audited if available)
   - Tax returns
   - Bank statements
   - Proof of matching funds

3. **Personal Documents** (for principals)
   - Resume/CV
   - Personal financial statement (sometimes)
   - Credit check authorization
   - Criminal record check (for some programs)

### Project-Specific Documents

**Depending on program type:**

1. **Technical Documents**
   - Technical specifications
   - Engineering drawings
   - Product prototypes
   - Research plan
   - Testing results

2. **Market Documents**
   - Market research reports
   - Customer letters of support
   - Sales agreements or letters of intent
   - Export orders
   - Marketing plan

3. **Planning Documents**
   - Business plan
   - Project plan with timeline
   - Risk assessment
   - Environmental impact assessment (if applicable)

4. **Quotes and Estimates**
   - Equipment quotes (usually 3 quotes for major purchases)
   - Service provider estimates
   - Contractor bids
   - Facility lease agreements

## Eligibility Verification

### Common Eligibility Criteria

**Verify before applying:**

1. **Business Type**
   - For-profit vs. non-profit eligibility
   - Incorporated vs. sole proprietor
   - Startup vs. established business
   - Size limits (employees, revenue)

2. **Location**
   - Physical presence in province/region
   - Operational headquarters location
   - Where work will be performed
   - Beneficiary location

3. **Industry/Sector**
   - Specific industries targeted
   - NAICS codes
   - Technology readiness level (for R&D)
   - Excluded sectors

4. **Project Stage**
   - Pre-commercial vs. commercial
   - Development stage requirements
   - Timeline restrictions
   - Previous funding limits

### Disqualification Factors

**Common reasons for ineligibility:**

- Outstanding government debts
- Previous funding violations
- Bankruptcy history
- Ownership by government entities
- Certain industries (e.g., tobacco, cannabis in some programs)
- Foreign-controlled businesses (for some programs)
- Already completed projects (retroactive funding rarely allowed)

## Application Process Tips

### Before You Apply

1. **Read Guidelines Thoroughly**
   - Every word of the program guide
   - Eligibility criteria
   - Evaluation criteria
   - Reporting requirements

2. **Contact Program Officer**
   - Ask questions before applying
   - Verify eligibility
   - Clarify requirements
   - Get feedback on concept (if allowed)

3. **Gather Information Early**
   - Start collecting documents weeks ahead
   - Request letters of support early
   - Get quotes well in advance
   - Prepare financial statements

4. **Use Provided Templates**
   - Don't create your own formats
   - Follow all instructions precisely
   - Use suggested page limits
   - Include all requested sections

### During Application

1. **Be Clear and Concise**
   - Answer questions directly
   - Use plain language
   - Avoid jargon unless necessary
   - Provide context for technical terms

2. **Provide Evidence**
   - Back up claims with data
   - Include relevant attachments
   - Reference supporting documents
   - Use credible sources

3. **Address Selection Criteria**
   - Read how applications are scored
   - Address each criterion explicitly
   - Show how your project meets requirements
   - Highlight strengths

4. **Proofread Thoroughly**
   - Check spelling and grammar
   - Verify all numbers are consistent
   - Ensure budget adds up correctly
   - Have someone else review

### Common Mistakes to Avoid

❌ **Don't:**
- Apply at the last minute
- Exceed page limits
- Skip required fields
- Submit incomplete applications
- Provide inconsistent information
- Forget to sign/authorize
- Miss supporting documents
- Use outdated templates

✅ **Do:**
- Start early
- Follow all instructions
- Double-check everything
- Keep copies of everything
- Track submission confirmation
- Note reference numbers
- Keep communication records
- Follow up if no response

## After Submission

### What to Expect

1. **Confirmation**
   - Immediate: Email confirmation of submission
   - Within days: Application received acknowledgment
   - Save all confirmation emails and reference numbers

2. **Review Process**
   - Completeness check (are all documents included?)
   - Eligibility screening (do you qualify?)
   - Technical/merit review (how strong is the application?)
   - Approval decision

3. **Timeline**
   - Varies widely by program (weeks to months)
   - Rolling intake: typically 4-12 weeks
   - Competition-based: 2-6 months
   - Check program guidelines for typical timelines

4. **Possible Outcomes**
   - Approved (may be full or partial funding)
   - Declined (with or without feedback)
   - Request for more information
   - Waitlisted (if oversubscribed)

### If Approved

1. **Funding Agreement**
   - Carefully review all terms and conditions
   - Understand reporting requirements
   - Note payment schedule
   - Ask questions about anything unclear

2. **Project Start**
   - Only start after agreement signed (usually)
   - Keep detailed records from day one
   - Track all expenses meticulously
   - Document milestones and outcomes

3. **Reporting**
   - Submit reports on time
   - Provide requested documentation
   - Track KPIs and metrics
   - Communicate changes proactively

4. **Compliance**
   - Follow funding agreement terms
   - Use funds only for approved purposes
   - Maintain separate accounting
   - Keep records for required retention period

### If Declined

1. **Request Feedback**
   - Most programs provide feedback if requested
   - Understand why application was unsuccessful
   - Ask what could be improved

2. **Review and Improve**
   - Address feedback for next attempt
   - Strengthen weak areas
   - Consider reapplying
   - Look for similar programs

3. **Appeal (if available)**
   - Some programs allow appeals
   - Check program guidelines
   - Must have grounds (error in process, new info, etc.)
   - Act within appeal period

- Budget worksheets
- Project plans
- Financial projections
- Impact assessments

### Professional Support

Consider hiring help for:
- **Grant writers:** Experienced in government applications
- **Accountants:** Financial statement preparation
- **Consultants:** Market research, business planning
- **Lawyers:** Contract review, IP strategy

## Key Takeaways

1. **Start early** - Applications take time to prepare properly
2. **Read everything** - Guidelines, criteria, FAQs
3. **Ask questions** - Program officers are there to help
4. **Be thorough** - Incomplete applications often get rejected
5. **Follow instructions** - Precisely, even if they seem excessive
6. **Keep records** - Document everything during and after
7. **Be honest** - Accuracy and transparency are critical
8. **Get help** - Don't be afraid to use professional support

---

**Remember:** Government funding is competitive but accessible. A well-prepared application significantly improves your chances of success.
